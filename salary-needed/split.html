How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Split: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Croatia's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in EUR for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Split?

To live comfortably in Split in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately €41,716. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is €20,858.

What is the cost of living in Split?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Split is approximately €551. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly €492. The effective tax rate is 40.0%.

Is €20,858 enough to live in Split?

€20,858 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Split — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least €41,716.
