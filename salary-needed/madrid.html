How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Madrid: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Spain's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in EUR for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Madrid?

To live comfortably in Madrid in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately €53,838. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is €24,453.

What is the cost of living in Madrid?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Madrid is approximately €933. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly €517. The effective tax rate is 35.4%.

Is €24,453 enough to live in Madrid?

€24,453 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Madrid — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least €53,838.
