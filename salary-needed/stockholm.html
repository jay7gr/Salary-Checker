How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Stockholm: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Sweden's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in SEK for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Stockholm?

To live comfortably in Stockholm in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately kr 867,106. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is kr 392,215.

What is the cost of living in Stockholm?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Stockholm is approximately kr 13,656. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly kr 6,282. The effective tax rate is 44.8%.

Is kr 392,215 enough to live in Stockholm?

kr 392,215 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Stockholm — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least kr 867,106.
