How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Geneva: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Switzerland's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions (including local taxes for Geneva). All figures are in CHF for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Geneva?

To live comfortably in Geneva in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately CHF 144,249. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is CHF 65,240.

What is the cost of living in Geneva?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Geneva is approximately CHF 2,107. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly CHF 1,070. The effective tax rate is 47.2%.

Is CHF 65,240 enough to live in Geneva?

CHF 65,240 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Geneva — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least CHF 144,249.
