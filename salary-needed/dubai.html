How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Dubai: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using UAE's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in AED for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Dubai?

To live comfortably in Dubai in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately AED 238,840. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is AED 119,420.

What is the cost of living in Dubai?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Dubai is approximately AED 6,521. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly AED 3,431. The effective tax rate is 0.0%.

Is AED 119,420 enough to live in Dubai?

AED 119,420 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Dubai — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least AED 238,840.
