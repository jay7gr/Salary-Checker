How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Panama City: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Panama's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in USD for 2026.

Is $22,844 enough to live in Panama City?
