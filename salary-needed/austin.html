How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Austin: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using United States's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions (including local taxes for Austin). All figures are in USD for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Austin?

To live comfortably in Austin in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately $89,582. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is $42,006.

What is the cost of living in Austin?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Austin is approximately $1,800. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly $1,032. The effective tax rate is 24.1%.

Is $42,006 enough to live in Austin?

$42,006 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Austin — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least $89,582.
