How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Bali (Denpasar): rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Indonesia's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in IDR for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Bali (Denpasar)?

To live comfortably in Bali (Denpasar) in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately Rp 348.4M. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is Rp 164.3M.

What is the cost of living in Bali (Denpasar)?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Bali (Denpasar) is approximately Rp 6.9M. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly Rp 4.5M. The effective tax rate is 21.1%.

Is Rp 164.3M enough to live in Bali (Denpasar)?

Rp 164.3M is the bare minimum salary to get by in Bali (Denpasar) — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least Rp 348.4M.
