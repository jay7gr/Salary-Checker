How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Shenzhen: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using China's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in CNY for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Shenzhen?

To live comfortably in Shenzhen in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately ¥371,582. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is ¥167,966.

What is the cost of living in Shenzhen?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Shenzhen is approximately ¥6,059. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly ¥3,400. The effective tax rate is 38.9%.

Is ¥167,966 enough to live in Shenzhen?

¥167,966 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Shenzhen — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least ¥371,582.
