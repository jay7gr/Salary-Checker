How We Calculate This

We start with actual monthly costs in Cairo: rent for a 1-bedroom apartment, groceries, utilities, transport, and healthcare. For the "comfortable" tier, we apply the 50/30/20 rule — your essentials should be 50% of take-home pay, leaving 30% for wants and 20% for savings.

We then reverse-calculate the gross (pre-tax) salary you'd need, using Egypt's progressive tax brackets and mandatory deductions. All figures are in EGP for 2026.

Frequently Asked Questions

What salary do you need to live in Cairo?

To live comfortably in Cairo in 2026, you need a gross annual salary of approximately E£869,967. This covers rent, groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare, and leaves 30% for wants and 20% for savings (50/30/20 rule). The minimum to get by is E£415,257.

What is the cost of living in Cairo?

Monthly rent for a 1-bedroom apartment in Cairo is approximately E£14,639. Essential monthly expenses (groceries, utilities, transport, healthcare) add roughly E£9,613. The effective tax rate is 33.1%.

Is E£415,257 enough to live in Cairo?

E£415,257 is the bare minimum salary to get by in Cairo — it covers rent and essential expenses with very little left over. For a comfortable lifestyle with savings, you'd want at least E£869,967.
