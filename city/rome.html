About Rome

Rome has a cost of living index of 49.3, making it a low cost city compared to New York (baseline = 100). It ranks #45 out of 101 cities tracked globally.

The local currency is EUR. A salary of $75,000 USD in New York would need to be approximately €31,351 in Rome to maintain the same purchasing power.

The approximate effective income tax rate in Italy is 25% for a mid-range earner. Including social security and other mandatory deductions, the total deduction rate is approximately 34.3%.

Our cost of living index (COLI) uses New York City as the baseline (COLI = 100). Indices are derived from multiple sources including Numbeo, Expatistan, and national statistics agencies. Exchange rates are sourced from the European Central Bank.

Tax rates represent approximate effective rates for mid-range earners based on OECD Taxing Wages data and do not constitute tax advice.
