Key Takeaways: Houston vs Tel Aviv

Overall, Houston is approximately 35% cheaper than Tel Aviv based on our cost of living index. Houston has a COLI of 60.2 (ranked #37 of 101 cities), while Tel Aviv has a COLI of 81.3 (ranked #10).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Houston averages $1,600/month compared to $1,800/month in Tel Aviv. That makes Houston approximately 12% cheaper for rent alone.

Houston has lower total deductions (19.1%) compared to Tel Aviv (28.5%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Houston. When evaluating a relocation, remember that total deductions directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.
