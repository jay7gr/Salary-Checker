Key Takeaways: Berlin vs Prague

Overall, Prague is approximately 40% cheaper than Berlin based on our cost of living index. Berlin has a COLI of 51.2 (ranked #43 of 101 cities), while Prague has a COLI of 30.6 (ranked #71).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Berlin averages $1,200/month compared to $800/month in Prague. That makes Prague approximately 33% cheaper for rent alone.

Prague has a lower effective tax rate (15%) compared to Berlin (32%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Prague. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn €63,592 in Berlin, you would need approximately Kč 1,032,847 in Prague to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 40% less thanks to lower cost of living.
