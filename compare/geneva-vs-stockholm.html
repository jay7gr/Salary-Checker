Key Takeaways: Geneva vs Stockholm

Overall, Stockholm is approximately 46% cheaper than Geneva based on our cost of living index. Geneva has a COLI of 118.6 (ranked #2 of 101 cities), while Stockholm has a COLI of 64.3 (ranked #30).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Geneva averages $2,600/month compared to $1,500/month in Stockholm. That makes Stockholm approximately 42% cheaper for rent alone.

Geneva has a lower effective tax rate (18%) compared to Stockholm (32%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Geneva. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn CHF 60,768 in Geneva, you would need approximately kr 370,182 in Stockholm to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 46% less thanks to lower cost of living.
