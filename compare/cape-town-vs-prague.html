Key Takeaways: Cape Town vs Prague

Overall, Cape Town is approximately 8% cheaper than Prague based on our cost of living index. Cape Town has a COLI of 28.4 (ranked #76 of 101 cities), while Prague has a COLI of 30.6 (ranked #71).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Cape Town averages $650/month compared to $800/month in Prague. That makes Cape Town approximately 23% cheaper for rent alone.

Prague has a lower effective tax rate (15%) compared to Cape Town (21%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Prague. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn R 1,366,717 in Cape Town, you would need approximately Kč 1,862,034 in Prague to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 8% more due to higher cost of living.
