Key Takeaways: Porto vs Rome

Overall, Porto is approximately 64% cheaper than Rome based on our cost of living index. Porto has a COLI of 30.1 (ranked #73 of 101 cities), while Rome has a COLI of 49.3 (ranked #45).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Porto averages $800/month compared to $1,200/month in Rome. That makes Porto approximately 50% cheaper for rent alone.

Porto has a lower effective tax rate (25%) compared to Rome (31%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Porto. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn €63,592 in Porto, you would need approximately €104,155 in Rome to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 64% more due to higher cost of living.
