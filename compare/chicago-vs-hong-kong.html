Key Takeaways: Chicago vs Hong Kong

Overall, Chicago is approximately 1% cheaper than Hong Kong based on our cost of living index. Chicago has a COLI of 73.2 (ranked #19 of 101 cities), while Hong Kong has a COLI of 73.6 (ranked #18).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Chicago averages $2,000/month compared to $2,500/month in Hong Kong. That makes Chicago approximately 25% cheaper for rent alone.

Hong Kong has a lower effective tax rate (15%) compared to Chicago (24%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Hong Kong. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn $75,000 in Chicago, you would need approximately HK$590,559 in Hong Kong to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 1% more due to higher cost of living.
