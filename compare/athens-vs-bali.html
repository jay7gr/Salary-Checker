Key Takeaways: Athens vs Bali (Denpasar)

Overall, Bali (Denpasar) is approximately 17% cheaper than Athens based on our cost of living index. Athens has a COLI of 30.6 (ranked #70 of 101 cities), while Bali (Denpasar) has a COLI of 25.3 (ranked #84).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Athens averages $700/month compared to $450/month in Bali (Denpasar). That makes Bali (Denpasar) approximately 36% cheaper for rent alone.

Bali (Denpasar) has a lower effective tax rate (15%) compared to Athens (22%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Bali (Denpasar). When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn €63,592 in Athens, you would need approximately Rp 957,229,654 in Bali (Denpasar) to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 17% less thanks to lower cost of living.
