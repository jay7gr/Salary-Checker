Key Takeaways: Porto vs Santiago

Overall, Porto is approximately 10% cheaper than Santiago based on our cost of living index. Porto has a COLI of 30.1 (ranked #73 of 101 cities), while Santiago has a COLI of 33.2 (ranked #62).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Porto averages $800/month compared to $550/month in Santiago. That makes Santiago approximately 31% cheaper for rent alone.

Santiago has a lower effective tax rate (13%) compared to Porto (25%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Santiago. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn €63,592 in Porto, you would need approximately CLP 77,553,987 in Santiago to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 10% more due to higher cost of living.
