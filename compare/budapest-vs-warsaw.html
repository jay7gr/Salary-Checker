Key Takeaways: Budapest vs Warsaw

Overall, Budapest is approximately 9% cheaper than Warsaw based on our cost of living index. Budapest has a COLI of 26.1 (ranked #80 of 101 cities), while Warsaw has a COLI of 28.4 (ranked #75).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Budapest averages $600/month compared to $700/month in Warsaw. That makes Budapest approximately 17% cheaper for rent alone.

Warsaw has lower total deductions (21.4%) compared to Budapest (33.5%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Warsaw. When evaluating a relocation, remember that total deductions directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.
