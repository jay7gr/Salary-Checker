Key Takeaways: Boston vs Madrid

Overall, Madrid is approximately 54% cheaper than Boston based on our cost of living index. Boston has a COLI of 82.4 (ranked #9 of 101 cities), while Madrid has a COLI of 38.0 (ranked #57).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Boston averages $2,800/month compared to $1,100/month in Madrid. That makes Madrid approximately 61% cheaper for rent alone.

Boston has a lower effective tax rate (14%) compared to Madrid (22%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Boston. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn $75,000 in Boston, you would need approximately €29,326 in Madrid to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 54% less thanks to lower cost of living.
