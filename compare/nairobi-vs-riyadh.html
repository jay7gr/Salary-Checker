Key Takeaways: Nairobi vs Riyadh

Overall, Nairobi is approximately 72% cheaper than Riyadh based on our cost of living index. Nairobi has a COLI of 24.7 (ranked #86 of 101 cities), while Riyadh has a COLI of 42.6 (ranked #51).

When it comes to housing, one-bedroom apartment rent in Nairobi averages $500/month compared to $800/month in Riyadh. That makes Nairobi approximately 60% cheaper for rent alone.

Riyadh has a lower effective tax rate (0%) compared to Nairobi (27%), meaning you keep more of your gross salary in Riyadh. Saudi Arabia is a tax-free jurisdiction, which is a significant financial advantage. When evaluating a relocation, remember that tax rates directly impact your take-home pay and should be weighed alongside cost of living differences.

Salary Equivalent

If you earn KSh 9,685,617 in Nairobi, you would need approximately SAR 485,071 in Riyadh to maintain the same purchasing power. That is 72% more due to higher cost of living.
